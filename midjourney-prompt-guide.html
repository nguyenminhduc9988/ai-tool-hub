The Ultimate Midjourney Prompt Guide: Create Stunning AI Art

Published June 2026 · AI Tool Hub

Mastering Midjourney Prompts

Midjourney produces breathtaking AI art, but only if you know how to prompt it effectively. This guide covers everything from basic syntax to advanced techniques that professional AI artists use daily.

Prompt Basics

A Midjourney prompt follows this structure:

/imagine [subject] [style] [details] [parameters]

The Formula That Works

The most effective prompts include these elements:

  1. Subject: What is the image about? Be specific.
  2. Medium: Photography, oil painting, digital art, watercolor, etc.
  3. Style: Cinematic, minimalist, photorealistic, anime, etc.
  4. Lighting: Golden hour, neon, studio lighting, natural, etc.
  5. Color: Vibrant, muted, monochrome, pastel, etc.
  6. Composition: Close-up, wide angle, bird's eye, etc.
  7. Parameters: --ar, --v, --s, --q, etc.

Essential Parameters

ParameterWhat It DoesExample

Advanced Techniques

Multi-Prompting

Use weights to control the importance of elements:

landscape::1.5 with a castle::0.8 and a dragon::0.3 in the sky::0.5

Image Blending

Blend two images by pasting their URLs:

/blend [image1 URL] [image2 URL]

Style References

Use a reference image to match its style:

your prompt --sref [image URL] --sw 1000

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Too vague: "a beautiful landscape" → "a misty mountain landscape at sunrise with a winding river"
  • Too many styles: Mixing 3+ art styles creates muddy results.
  • Ignoring aspect ratio: Wrong aspect ratio wastes image space.
  • Over-stylizing: --s 1000 can make images look artificial.

Conclusion

Great Midjourney art comes from specific, well-structured prompts. Use the formula, study the parameters, and iterate quickly. The more you practice, the better your results will be.
